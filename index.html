Bio

I am a fourth-year EECS Ph.D. student in the Electromechanical Systems Group at MIT. I'm interested in the full stack of sensing, signal processing, and data mining for energy systems, especially microgrids. My work has focused on modelling and diagnostics for microgrid power systems with an emphasis on minimal sensor count.

In my free time, I enjoy running, reading, and tinkering with Linux-based operating systems.

I received the Master of Science degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from MIT in 2022. I graduated from Auburn University in 2018 with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and a minor in German.
