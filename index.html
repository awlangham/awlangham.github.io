Bio

I am a third-year EECS Ph.D. student in the Electromechanical Systems Group at MIT. I'm interested in signal processing, sensor development, and computer systems for energy management and sustainability. In particular, I work on nonintrusive load monitoring (NILM), in which a single set of current and voltage sensors can disaggregate the energy consumed by a power grid to each load using signal processing and machine learning techniques.

In my free time, I enjoy running, reading, and tinkering with Linux-based operating systems.

I received the Master of Science degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from MIT in 2022. I graduated from Auburn University in 2018 with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and a minor in German.
